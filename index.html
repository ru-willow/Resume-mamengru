Mengru Ma (马梦茹)

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher and Assistant Research Fellow at the School of Artificial Intelligence, Xidian University, advised by Prof. Licheng Jiao. I am also a member of the Key Laboratory of Intelligent Perception and Image Understanding of Ministry of Education. My research focuses on multimodal large models, multi-source remote sensing image fusion and interpretation, image-text retrieval, video generation, panoramic reconstruction, and vision-language grounding.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Technology from Xidian University in December 2024, supervised by Prof. Licheng Jiao. Prior to that, I received my B.Eng. in Communication Engineering from Hebei University of Engineering in 2019.

Research Interests

Multimodal Remote Sensing Fusion & Interpretation

Multi-source remote sensing image fusion, multimodal representation learning, hyperspectral and SAR image joint interpretation, cross-modal feature enhancement.

Representative works: Information Fusion 2023, IEEE TMM 2025, IEEE TGRS 2025.

Vision-Language Grounding & Generation

Image-text retrieval, referring segmentation and visual grounding collaboration, text-to-video generation with temporal-consistent state modeling.

Representative works: IEEE TCSVT 2024, IEEE TGRS 2025.

Brain-Inspired Learning & Cross-View Reconstruction

Brain-inspired perception and cognition, cross-view panoramic reconstruction from satellite imagery, multi-scale geometric alignment.

Representative survey: IEEE TNNLS 2024.

Future Research Directions

  1. Image Fusion & Interpretation in Extreme Environments: Fully exploit source image information to address challenges such as nighttime, rain/fog, over-exposure, and under-exposure.
  2. Referring Remote Sensing Understanding via Segmentation-Grounding Synergy: Break through from micro-scale cross-modal feature enhancement and coarse-to-fine hierarchical reasoning with "localize first, verify later" strategy.
  3. State-consistent Text-to-Video Generation: Explicitly model semantic, appearance, and motion states during inference to improve spatiotemporal consistency and stability.
  4. Cross-view Panoramic Reconstruction from Satellite Imagery: Explore multi-scale feature alignment, cross-view geometric relationship modeling, and semantic consistency constraints.
